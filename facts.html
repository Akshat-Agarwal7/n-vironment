Plastic Waste Facts:-

  • Staggering Production: Over 300 million tons of plastic are produced globally each year, and this number is expected to double within the next two decades.

  • Single-Use Plastics: Approximately 50% of the plastic produced is for single-use purposes, such as packaging and disposable items.

  • Limited Recycling: Only about 9% of all plastic waste ever produced has been recycled. The majority ends up in landfills, incinerators, or in the natural environment.

  • Marine Pollution: An estimated 8 million tons of plastic enter the world's oceans every year, harming marine life and ecosystems.

  • Microplastics Everywhere: Microplastics, tiny plastic particles less than 5mm in size, have been found in the most remote areas of the planet, including deep-sea trenches and polar ice.

  • Long Decomposition Time: Plastic bottles, on average, take 450 years to decompose, while fishing lines can take up to 600 years.

  • Toxic Impact: Plastics can leach harmful chemicals into the environment, posing health risks to both wildlife and humans.

  • Landfill Overflow: Landfills are overflowing with plastic waste, contributing to soil pollution and releasing harmful substances into the ground.

  • Impact on Wildlife: Thousands of marine animals and seabirds die each year due to ingestion of or entanglement in plastic debris.

  • Global Problem: Plastic pollution is a global issue that affects every country, from densely populated urban areas to remote islands.
